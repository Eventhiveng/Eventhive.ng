Bringing Together Nigeria Telecoms industry

The Nigeria Telecoms Forum unites industry experts to address challenges and opportunities in Nigeria’s telecom sector.

Get Ticket

Stay ahead of Nigeria's telecoms market.

Nigeria’s telecoms industry has widely been known as a major contributor to the country’s GDP, as the industry grapple with multiple challenges which includes challenging operating environment and currency depreciation, stakeholders will converge at the Nigeria Telecoms Forum to debate and define the future of the industry.

Focused on exploring the future of Nigeria’s telecoms industry, the event will bring together top executives across various verticals of the telecoms industry. Cybersecurity and emerging technologies, infrastructure and networks, power reliability, regulation and investment, inflation and economic market realities will headline discussions at the forum.

Fixed line and mobile carriers, ISPs, regulators, investors, technology suppliers, consultants, telecoms executives, telco associations, government representatives amongst other players will converge to share insights, expertise and strategies that will spur the growth of the telecom industry in Nigeria.

Agenda & Key Discussions

Navigating the Digital Divide: Challenges and Opportunities in Nigeria's Telecoms Industry

This panel session will delve into the critical role of telecommunications in driving Nigeria's digital transformation. Panelists will discuss the challenges and opportunities facing the industry, including infrastructure development, affordability, and digital inclusion. Key discussion areas will include:

  • Infrastructure development: Examining the state of Nigeria's telecom infrastructure, identifying gaps, and exploring strategies for expansion and modernization.
  • Economic realities: Addressing the impact of inflation and FX volatilities on the operations of telcos.
  • Affordability and accessibility: Addressing the issue of affordability and ensuring that telecom services are accessible to all segments of the population.
  • Regulatory framework: The role of regulators in fostering a conducive environment for innovation and investment in the telecoms sector.
  • Cybersecurity and data privacy: Protecting critical infrastructure and safeguarding consumer data in an increasingly interconnected world.
  • Emerging technologies: Leveraging emerging technologies like 5G, IoT, and AI to drive innovation and improve service delivery.

Financing the Digital Future: Funding Telecom Infrastructure in Nigeria

This panel session will explore the critical issue of financing telecom infrastructure in Nigeria. With the increasing demand for connectivity and digital services, securing adequate funding is essential for the growth and development of the telecommunications sector. Panelists will discuss innovative funding models, regulatory frameworks, risk mitigation strategies, and international partnerships to drive infrastructure investment.

Focus discussion areas include:

  • Innovative funding models and investment opportunities: Exploringalternativefundingsourcessuch as public-private partnerships, infrastructure funds, and debt financing.
  • Regulatory framework: The role of government policies and regulations in attracting investment and stimulating infrastructure development.
  • Risk mitigation: Strategies for mitigating financial and operational risks associated with large-scale infrastructure projects.
  • Regulatory framework: The role of regulators in fostering a conducive environment for innovation and investment in the telecoms sector.
  • International partnerships: The potential for international collaboration and partnerships to drive infrastructure development.
  • Sustainability and environmental impact: Balancing the need for infrastructure development with environmental sustainability and social responsibility.

From Connectivity to Computing: How Telcos are making inroads into the data centre market

This panel session will explore the evolving role of telecommunications companies (telcos) as they venture into the data center market. As traditional carriers of data, telcos are now expanding their offerings to become providers of critical digital infrastructure. Panelists will discuss the strategic motivations behind this shi􏰀, the challenges and opportunities involved, and the impact on the broader technology landscape.

Key discussion areas will include:

  • Leveraging network infrastructure: How telcos can leverage their existing network infrastructure to build and operate efficient data centers.
  • Expanding offerings: Exploring the potential for telcos to offer additional value-added services, such as cloud computing, cybersecurity, and managed services.
  • Partnerships and collaborations: The importance of strategic partnerships with other players in the ecosystem, including hyperscalers, cloud service providers, and IT companies.
  • Regulatory and policy considerations: Navigating the regulatory landscape and addressing policy challenges to facilitate data center development.
  • Financial models and investment strategies: Identifying sustainable financing models and investment strategies for data center projects.
  • Customer focus and innovation: Meeting the evolving needs of customers and driving innovation in data center services.

Meeting the evolving needs of customers and driving innovation in data center services.

This panel session will delve into the evolving role of telecommunications companies in Nigeria, exploring the opportunities beyond traditional connectivity services. As the digital landscape continues to expand, telcos are positioned to play a pivotal role in driving economic growth and social development.

Key discussion areas will include:

  • Digital Services: Exploring the potential of telcos to offer a range of digital services, such as fintech, e- commerce, and cloud computing.
  • Emerging technologies: Examining the place of emerging technologies such as 5G technology, Internet of Things (IoT), and artificial intelligence (AI) in shaping innovation in the telco industry.
  • Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS): Leveraging existing infrastructure to provide data center and cloud services to businesses.
  • Cybersecurity and data privacy: Addressing the growing cybersecurity threats and protecting sensitive customer data.
  • Regulatory framework: The need for supportive regulatory policies to foster innovation and investment in the telecoms sector.
  • Partnerships and collaborations: Exploring strategic partnerships with other industry players to accelerate digital transformation.
