About Nigeria Power Outlook

Eventhive is delighted to announce that Nigeria Power Outlook 2024 will hold on the 25th of April, 2024. Now in its 5th year, Nigeria Power Outlook is a high caliber Nigeria power-focused conference, exhibitions and networking platform.

The event annually provides a veritable platform that convenes stakeholders, developers and investors in the power sector (on-grid and off-grid), representatives from state and federal government of Nigeria, Nigeria Electricity Regulatory Commission (NERC), Independent Power Producers (IPP), Distribution companies, Development partners, Renewable energy companies, amongst other stakeholders working within the power industry.

Nigeria Power Outlook will harmonize public and private sector stakeholders to define the future of the power industry, debate and discuss key developments in the power industry, share knowledge and build collaborative approaches with the common objective to increase access, reliability and stability of power supply, market certainty and viability of the power sector.

Nigeria Power Outlook will combine conference and exhibitions, beyond the conversations and networking, the event will provide a platform for companies working in the power value chain, to showcase their solutions, through available exhibition opportunities.

Nigeria Power Outlook is annually organized by Eventhive.ng, West Africa’s leading B2B event serving company, with event portfolio’s across Power, Oil & Gas,Tech, Real Estate and Transport in Nigeria, UK and USA.

Agenda & Key Discussions

  1. State of the market: Examining how The Nigeria
    Electricity Act 2023 will transform Nigeria's Power Sector

    This session will bring together industry leaders, executives and stakeholders working across various verticals of the Nigerian electricity value chain, providing them with an opportunity to examine the Electricity Act 2023 and what it means for the future of Nigeria's power sector.

  2. Mobilising private capital for clean energy in Nigeria

    This session will examine conversations across a range of issues surrounding the provision and availability of equity, debt, grant finance and other options available for DFIs to scale investment across clean energy in Nigeria and closing the financing gap.

  3. Gas-To-Power-The race towards Net Zero economy in Nigeria

    This session will assess the global gas market and what it means for Nigeria's domestic gas and power markets. Speakers will explore gas-to-power as a pathway to decarbonisation.

  4. In-Conversation with DISCO's

    This session will bring together, players across Nigeria's electricity distribution companies, providing them with an opportunity to provide updates on liquidity issues, smart metering, investment efforts and the role, The Electricity Act 2023 will play in their industry.

  5. Executive Conversation - What next for Infrastructural Roadmap for Nigeria's Power Sector

  6. Leveraging digital technology to deliver value to customers in Nigeria's electricity value chain
