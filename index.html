April, 2021.

Nigeria’s potential to become one of the World’s largest economies will remain just an aspiration without the electricity required to pursue aggressive industrialization, including the revitalization of moribund local industries. The country’s power sector is undoubtedly invaluable to unlocking the potential and positioning her on the path to sustainable growth.

Eventhive.ng is hosting 2nd edition of the annual Nigeria Power Outlook themed ‘THE PATHWAY FOR GROWTH’. Nigeria Power Outlook is the foremost gathering of the Industry’s finest minds, top-level business leaders and decision makers across government, power and energy companies, development agencies, commercial banks, development finance institutions e.t.c, providing them with an opportunity to discuss, share thoughts on latest industry challenges & solutions, network and chart a growth path for the power and energy industry.


This year’s edition will build on the impactful conversations at the 2020 edition, which had the theme ‘FUTURE OF POWER- CONNECTING POLICIES TO REAL TIME POWER SOLUTIONS’. Nigeria Power Outlook, now in its 2nd edition, will provide an opportunity for learning, knowledge transfer, interaction and peer-to-peer networking. The event will unite stakeholders from Power value chain which includes Generation, Transmission, Distribution, Renewable Energy, Off-takers, Developers, Equipment Manufacturers, Technology providers, Legal and Consulting companies.

Nigeria Power Outlook offers a veritable platform for exhibitors to showcase the latest brands, products, technologies and innovations to a range of individuals from the Power and Energy ecosystem. Nigeria Power Outlook is annually organized by Eventhive.ng, West Africa’s leading PR Firm with core competencies in developing Africa’s businesses via face-to-face/virtual events.


WHY YOU SHOULD BE PART OF NIGERIA POWER OUTLOOK

Nigeria Power Outlook will create an opportunity to;

Network with Industry Leaders in the Power and Energy Sector.

Explore business opportunities within the Power sector.

KEY FOCUS AREAS

  • De-risking Energy Investment in the Post-Pandemic Environment.
  • The appeal and potential of off-grid power projects.
  • Attracting and retaining investments in the Nigerian Power and Gas Sectors.
  • Improving Power Delivery across the Generation, Transmission and Distribution Value Chain.
  • Understanding the Regulatory Environment.
  • Taking the grid forward : Sustainable approaches for getting the grid back to viability and encouraging private investments.
  • Collaborative approaches to ending energy poverty.
  • Nigeria’s LPG Market; Unlocking barriers to private capital- Accelerating market growth.
  • Metering, Transmission and Distribution in Nigeria’s Power Sector (CASE STUDY)
  • Mobilizing long term domestic savings to fund Nigeria’s power and energy infrastructure.
