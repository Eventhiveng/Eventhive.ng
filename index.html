Best of Nigeria's banking industry? Look no further

Nigeria’s financial services sector is entering a transformative phase led by recapitalisation reforms, rapid digitisation, and the increasing influence of AI, open banking, and fintech collaboration. Banks are navigating an environment of high interest rates, inflation, currency volatility, and shifting consumer expectations.

Returning for its second edition in 2026, the Nigeria Banking Outlook will convene CEOs, regulators, fintech leaders, investors, and analysts to explore strategies for stability, innovation, and growth in a redefined banking landscape. Discussions will focus on how institutions can adapt business models, strengthen risk management, and leverage technology to create more inclusive financial ecosystems.

The Nigeria Banking Outlook 2026 will provide a 360° view of the country’s financial future, from capital reform to digital transformation. By convening thought leaders and innovators, the event will advance a shared vision for a resilient, technology-driven, and inclusive banking system that underpins Nigeria’s economic growth.

Recapitalisation and the Road Ahead: Building a Stronger, More Competitive Banking Sector

With the CBN’s recapitalisation deadline drawing closer, Nigeria’s banking industry faces a pivotal test of resilience. This panel will analyze how the new capital requirements will reshape competition, governance, and financial stability across the sector. Discussions will also cover how banks can raise the necessary capital through equity, debt, and M&A, while maintaining profitability and public trust.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Capital Raising: Pathways for equity issuance, debt capital, and private placements.
  • Consolidation Trends: Impact of mergers and acquisitions on competitiveness.
  • SME and Infrastructure Lending: How stronger banks can enhance credit creation.
  • Regulatory Oversight: CBN’s strategy for phased compliance and sector balance.
  • Investor Confidence: Transparency, disclosure, and governance reform.

AI, Data, and the Future of Banking: Unlocking Smarter Financial Systems

AI and machine learning are reshaping banking worldwide. Nigerian banks are adopting these technologies to enhance operational eﬃciency, improve fraud detection, and personalize financial services. This panel explores how AI can drive the next frontier of financial inclusion and risk intelligence, while balancing innovation with ethical and regulatory safeguards.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Operational Eﬃciency: Using AI to streamline workflows and cut costs.
  • Personalized Banking: Leveraging data for predictive insights and customer value creation.
  • Fraud Detection: Deploying AI to monitor anomalies and prevent real-time fraud.
  • Ethical AI and Compliance: Embedding governance and consumer protection frameworks.
  • Digital Talent: Building an AI-ready workforce through upskilling initiatives.

Financing Growth in a Volatile Economy: Reinventing Nigeria’s Trade Finance Landscape

Trade finance remains a critical lever for Nigeria’s economic growth, yet access to funding continues to be constrained by foreign exchange scarcity, regulatory bottlenecks, and credit risks. This panel will bring together bankers, trade experts, and policymakers to explore how Nigeria’s financial institutions can strengthen trade financing mechanisms to support exporters, importers, and SMEs operating within the AfCFTA framework.

Panelists will discuss how digital tools, correspondent banking relationships, and alternative financing models can mitigate risks and improve liquidity in cross-border trade. The session will also highlight opportunities for innovation through supply-chain financing, export credit guarantees, and regional payment systems. Key Focus Areas:

  • FX and Liquidity Challenges: Managing trade flows amid currency pressures.
  • rade Credit and Risk Mitigation: Leveraging guarantees, insurance, and structured products.
  • Digital Trade Platforms: Using new technologies to simplify trade processes.
  • AfCFTA Integration: Harnessing regional trade opportunities and cross-border banking frameworks.
  • SME Export Support: Expanding access to trade finance for smaller businesses.
  • Partnership Models: Collaboration between banks, DFIs, and export agencies.
