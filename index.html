Harnessing Africa's Mineral Wealth for a Sustainable Future

Africa holds significant reserves of critical minerals, including 48.1% of the world’s cobalt and 47.7% of its manganese, essential for the global energy transition. These resources present opportunities to generate revenue, create jobs, and improve living standards across the continent. However, Africa has yet to fully harness its resource potential.

The Africa Mining Investment Outlook 2025 will address challenges and opportunities in the mining industry, exploring how to leverage critical minerals for sustainable growth. The event will focus on attracting investments, ensuring responsible mining, and integrating these resources into Africa’s energy transition efforts.

Bringing together mining executives, government representatives, and industry leaders, the event will drive conversations on sustainable practices, innovation, and technology in mining. It will highlight the role of critical minerals in fostering energy independence while ensuring long-term benefits for the continent.

STATE OF THE MARKET - Industry report for Africa's Mining

Africa's Mining Industry and its role in Global energy transition

As we acceslerate towards cleaner energy solutions, Africa's mining will play a pivotal role in the global energy transition, with growing demand for important minerals like cobalt, lithium and copper. This session will explore trends shaping the future of Africa's mining industry with key spotlights on energy transition.

Capital at Play; Making African mining more attractive to investments

This session seeks to understand what incentives are required to enhance investor interest in Africa's mining industry, industry players will examine the broad spectrum of investment opportunities within Africa's critical minerals and rare earth projects. It will focus on creating an enabling environment that attracts key producers and buyers, as well as navigating the competitive landscape and exploring collaborative approaches among governments, private sector players and international partners.



This session will equally explore financing instruments foreign partners can provide to supply greater sums o f concessional financing and to de-risk private sector investments with a key focus on making Africa the world's mineral partner of choice.

Driving the growth of Africa's mining through Innovation

Engagements on this panel will gather key industry leaders to discuss how innovative strategies, next-generation technologies and sustainable practices are reshaping the African mining landscape. Panelists will explore policy reforms, digital tools and advanced processing techniques that can unlock Africa's mineral potential, boost value addition and position the continent as a global leader in critical minerals.



Speakers on the session will equally share insights on leveraging data for stronger ESG in Africa's mining industry.

Responsible Mining and its impact on the community

This session will examine how Africa's mining is impacting the host communities with a key focus on understanding the long-term commitments to these communities,the session will make a case for affected communities and the need for exploring post-mining opportunities.
