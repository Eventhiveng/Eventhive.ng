BRINGING NIGERIA
CLOSER TO THE WORLD

Nigeria and Africa at large, are confronted by multiple challenges which are tied to inability to attract and retain investments across their most important industries. Months into a new leadership, the country is witnessing implementation of a raft of reforms long sought by investors, bankers and business stakeholders to revive Africa’s biggest economy. In a bid to set the investment agenda for the new leadership, We are delighted to announce the return of the 3rd edition of the UK-Nigeria Business Outlook.

This year’s edition of the event will create a platform for partnerships and collaboration amongst Uk and Nigerian businesses. With investments at its core, The event will focus on those who are investing in the country’s future, highlighting the opportunities and obstacles that are impeding investments. Trade & Investments, Energy, Fintech and the Creative economy are expected to headline discussions at this year’s edition of the event scheduled to hold on the 18th of March, 2024 at Addleshaw Goddard, London.

UK-Nigeria Business Outlook will harmonize leading figures across Nigeria and abroad who are defining the future of the country’s future via their investment decisions whilst placing a laser focus on some of the opportunities across the focus sectors. CEO’s from multiple industries, development partners, investors and government stakeholders will gather under one roof to learn, engage, network and do business.

Attendees will engage with industry experts across the UK and Nigeria, discussing current export trends, investment bottlenecks, barriers to trade in Nigeria and what can be done to unlock Nigeria’s trade and investments.

The event is annually organized by Eventhive.ng, West Africa’s leading B2B event servicing company with event portfolios across Oil & Gas, Power, Transport, Real Estate, Tech, Fintech, Tech, Agribusiness across Nigeria, UK and US.

AGENDA AND
KEY DISCUSSIONS

  • UK-Nigeria Partnership;
    Update on the UK Government Trade and Investments Policy Priorities in Africa.
  • Investors’s Dialogue;
    How are investors responding to reforms in Nigeria?
    This session will bring together venture capitalists, institutional investors, financial organizations, business leaders, amongst others to discuss some of the recently implemented reforms and how they will impact how investors will view Nigeria.
  • Energy transition and Nigerian Gas;
    Debates have continued to rage around gas, this has spurred the need for reassessments of the role of natural gas in both energy transition and energy security, this session will draw investors and energy leaders across the UK and Nigeria who will shape conversations across accelerating investments into Nigeria’s natural gas and capital intensive infrastructure projects
  • Hear from Nigerian Government;
    Exclusive presentations and one-on-one engagements with Nigerian governments representatives, on the country’s policies and investment opportunities.
  • Creative Economy;
    Armed with over 200 Million people, Nigeria has the potential to further harness and maximize its creative industries. This session will bring together multiple creative leaders across media, entertainment, lifestyle, fashion, tourism and hospitality to explore strategies for developments of Nigeria’s creativity ecosystem.
  • Fintech without borders;
    This session will explore strategies for the inclusive and sustainable development of Fintech in Africa, with keen interest in cross-border payments and the role the industry will play in AFCTA. This panel will bring together leaders across various elements of Africa’s fintech.
